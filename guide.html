Setting up your PIN

The very first time you open Clarity, you'll see the PIN screen. No data has been saved yet — this is where you create your personal access code.

What you'll see
CLARITY

Create a 4-digit PIN
Tap four numbers on the keypad. You'll be asked to confirm them once. After that, you'll need your PIN every time you open Clarity.
Your PIN is stored only on this device. If you want to use Clarity on your iPhone AND your laptop, you'll set a PIN on each device separately. Keep them the same to avoid confusion!

Switching time periods

At the top of your dashboard, you can switch how your budget is displayed. Same data — just shown as weekly, fortnightly, monthly, or yearly amounts.

Period buttons
Weekly Fortnightly Monthly Quarterly Yearly
All amounts automatically convert when you switch. Your rent of $1,912/month becomes $956/fortnight.
If you get paid fortnightly, set the period to Fortnightly. Then every number you see is exactly what comes out of each pay. Makes budgeting feel real.
Utility bills (electricity, gas) are charged quarterly. Clarity stores them as monthly averages and automatically converts to whatever period you're viewing. The small hint text shows the original quarterly amount.

Each expense has a small tick button on the right. Tap it after you pay a bill to mark it as done for this pay period.

Marking something as paid doesn't remove it from your totals — it just helps you track what's been paid this cycle vs what's still outstanding.
5
Editing your budget figures

Your dashboard comes pre-loaded with your figures. If anything changes — new bill amount, new plan, new expense — you can edit it directly.

Click on any amount to edit it. Press Enter or click away to save. The dashboard updates instantly.
To add a new expense to any category, scroll to that section and click the "+ Add item" button at the bottom of the category.
All changes save automatically to your browser. They'll still be there next time you open Clarity on the same device.
6
Importing transactions from your bank

You can import a CSV file from your bank to auto-update your expenses. Clarity reads Australian bank formats and sorts transactions into categories automatically.

How to download your CSV from your bank:

CommBank (CBA)
NetBank → Accounts → Export transactions → Select date range → CSV
ANZ
ANZ Internet Banking → Accounts → Export → CSV format
Westpac / Bank of Melbourne
Online Banking → Statements → Download → CSV
NAB
NAB Internet Banking → Accounts → Download transactions → CSV
Once you have the CSV file, open Clarity, scroll to the Import CSV section, and drag your file onto it (or click to browse). Clarity will automatically match transactions to your expense categories.
Clarity reads and processes the file locally on your device. Your bank data never leaves your browser.
7
Installing on your iPhone

You can add Clarity to your iPhone home screen so it opens like a real app — full screen, no browser bar, with the Whetū Digital icon.

Open Safari on your iPhone (must be Safari, not Chrome)
Go to deep21star.github.io/clarity
Enter your PIN to make sure it's working
Tap the Share button at the bottom of Safari (the square with an arrow pointing up)
Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
Name it "Clarity" and tap Add
Find the Clarity icon on your home screen. Tap it — it opens full-screen, just like any other app.
Once installed on your iPhone, this is your go-to. Check it after each pay day — mark bills as paid, check your balance, stay on top of things. Takes 30 seconds.

Frequently Asked Questions
I forgot my PIN. How do I reset it?
Contact your Whetū Digital advisor (Deep) — he can reset it for you. In a future update, there will be a self-serve PIN reset option.
My data on my laptop doesn't show on my iPhone. Is that normal?
Yes, for now. Your data is saved per device. Use one device as your main (your iPhone is best). Cloud sync across devices is coming in a future update — it'll be automatic when it arrives.
Is my data safe? Who can see it?
Your data is stored only in your browser on your device — nobody else can access it. The PIN prevents anyone who picks up your phone from opening the dashboard. Your actual bank account details are never entered or stored in Clarity.
The electricity/gas amounts look wrong. How do I update them?
The amounts start as estimates based on your last few bills. When you get a new bill, click the amount in the dashboard and type in the real figure. It saves instantly.
Can I use this on my Android phone too?
Yes! Open your browser, go to the Clarity URL, and use it just like on your laptop. The "Add to Home Screen" option is available in Chrome on Android too.
How do I change the currency from AUD?
There's a currency selector at the top right of your dashboard. You can switch between AUD, NZD, USD, GBP, and EUR at any time.
Something doesn't look right — what do I do?
Message Deep at Whetū Digital. He'll sort it out, usually within a day. That's what the ongoing support is for.
