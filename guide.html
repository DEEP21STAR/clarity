💡How HEALTH is calculated: Clarity looks at your savings rate (what's left after expenses ÷ income).

✓ HEALTHY — saving 20% or more of your income
⚠ WARNING — saving 5–19% (could be tighter)
⚠ TIGHT — saving less than 5% but not in deficit
✕ DEFICIT — spending more than you earn

The goal is to get to HEALTHY. Even small improvements each month add up.
💡Period buttons + bill frequency — how they work together: Each bill has its own frequency (weekly, monthly, etc.). When you switch periods, Clarity converts all bills automatically. Example: a $1,200 Monthly bill shows as $600 in FORTNIGHTLY view and $300 in WEEKLY view. Your totals always reflect the period you're looking at.

📅 Setting a due date on a bill

Due dates power the Upcoming Bills strip (see Section 10). Set them once and Clarity reminds you automatically.

1
Find the bill you want to set a due date for in your dashboard.
2
Tap the bill row — a Due [date] badge appears beside the bill name. Tap it.
3
Enter the day of the month the bill is due (e.g. 15 for the 15th). Tap Save.
4
The bill will now appear in the Upcoming Bills strip as the due date approaches — amber within 3 days, red if overdue.

🧮 CALCULATOR

Quick tax and GST calculations — set to Australia by default. No internet needed, works offline.

💡The Income Tax tab shows your exact take-home pay after tax, Medicare levy, and super. Enter your gross salary and it breaks it all down instantly.
1
Tap 🧮 CALCULATOR in the nav bar at the top.
2
Choose a tab — Income Tax, GST, Tax Return, Year-on-Year, or Super.
3
Type in your numbers — results appear instantly. The 🇦🇺 / 🇳🇿 toggle switches between Australian and New Zealand tax rules.
→INCOME TAXEnter gross salary → see take-home pay, tax, Medicare levy, and super broken down
→GSTEnter an amount → add 10% GST or strip it out (AU 10% / NZ 15%)
→TAX RETURNEnter income, deductions, and tax paid → see if you're getting a refund or owe more
→YEAR-ON-YEARCompare two financial years side by side — useful if your income changed significantly
→SUPER / KIWISAVEREnter salary and contribution rates → see employer + employee super projections

📷 RECEIPTS

Photograph or upload receipts to keep them safe — especially useful at tax time. Tracks warranty dates too.

✅Mark receipts as Tax Deductible and tap Export Tax Summary to get a ready-to-print report you can hand to your accountant.
1
Tap 📷 RECEIPTS in the nav bar, then tap the upload zone.
2
Choose a photo from your camera roll, take a new photo, or select a PDF receipt — all work fine.
3
Fill in the merchant name, amount, and category. Toggle Tax Deductible to green if it's a work expense.
4
Tap SAVE RECEIPT. It's stored securely on your device — no account needed.
5
When tax time comes, tap 📄 Export Tax Summary — a printable summary opens with all your deductible receipts listed.
→CATEGORYChoose from: Vehicle, Meals, Office Supplies, Equipment, Travel, Clothing, Utilities, Medical, Education, Other
→GST INCLUDEDToggle on if the receipt includes GST — Clarity will calculate the GST component separately in the tax summary
→WARRANTY DATEOptional — enter the warranty expiry date for appliances or electronics. Useful to check before a warranty claim
→TAX DEDUCTIBLEToggle on for work-related expenses — only these appear in your Export Tax Summary report

☁ BACKUP

Your data lives on your device — always keep a backup in case your phone is lost or replaced.

⚠️Important: Budget data and receipts are stored on your device only. If you clear your browser or get a new phone, they will be gone unless you have a backup.
1
Tap ☁ BACKUP in the nav bar.
2
Tap ⬇ Download Backup.zip — this saves all your data and receipts into one file.
3
Upload that ZIP to iCloud Drive, Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox — step-by-step guides for each are right there in the Backup tab.
4
If you ever need to restore: download the ZIP from your cloud, tap ↩ Restore from Backup, and select it. Everything comes back.
📅Good habit: Download a backup at the end of each month, especially after adding receipts. Takes less than 10 seconds.

📈 NET WORTH TRACKER

Your assets minus your liabilities — the single most important number in personal finance. Update it whenever your situation changes and save monthly snapshots to watch it grow over time.

✅Net worth = what you own minus what you owe. Even if it's negative right now, tracking it monthly shows the trend — and that trend is what matters.
1
Scroll past the savings goals on your dashboard to find the NET WORTH TRACKER panel.
2
Under Assets — enter your savings account balance, car value, or anything you own. Under Liabilities — enter credit card balances, loans, Afterpay etc.
3
Tap + Add Asset or + Add Liability to add more rows. Tap ✕ to remove one.
4
Once updated, tap 📸 SAVE SNAPSHOT — this records your net worth for this month. Do this once a month to build your history.
5
Your last 6 monthly snapshots appear at the bottom of the panel so you can see progress at a glance.
⚠️Net worth data is saved on your device. Include it in your regular Backup (Section 9c) so it's not lost.

📂 IMPORTING A BANK CSV

Most banks let you export your transactions as a CSV file. Clarity can read that file and help you import your regular bills.

1
Download a CSV export from your bank's website or app (usually found under Transactions → Export).
2
On your Clarity dashboard, tap 📂 IMPORT CSV. Drag your CSV file onto the drop zone, or tap to browse for it.
3
Clarity shows a preview of the transactions it detected. Review the list — deselect anything you don't want to import.
4
Tap Import to add the selected transactions as bills in your dashboard.
💡CSV import works best for recurring transactions (rent, power, subscriptions). One-off purchases don't need to be in Clarity — focus on your regular bills.

⬇ EXPORTING TO SPREADSHEET

Export your entire Clarity budget as a CSV spreadsheet — opens in Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers.

1
On your dashboard, tap ⬇ EXPORT CSV.
2
A file downloads automatically — open it in Excel or Google Sheets to view all your bills, amounts, frequencies, and paid status in a table.
💡The export is a great way to share your budget with a partner or financial advisor — or to keep a spreadsheet record alongside your regular ZIP backup.
